Collection of Meaningful Lego Sets

I have always enjoyed building Lego sets since childhood. As my kids grew up, they all enjoyed LEGO sets, and as an adult, I found myself back to building sets. My wife and I enjoy spending time together working on sets, either the same set or different ones.

Universal Motor Set

Technic | 8054 | 1989

This Technic set is one of the earlier sets I remember playing with as a kid. The motorized gears and axles were fun to play with, and I spent hours seeing what else I could do with them.

Ford Mustang

Icons | 10265 | 2019

The 1967 GT-500 Fastback is probably my favorite car of all time. The detail on this set was faithful to the original car.

Atari Video Computer System

Icons | 10306 | 2022

The Atari 2600 was the first video game system we had. I remember playing Space Invaders for hours and thinking it was the coolest thing in the world. LEGO nailed the detail. From the woodgrain panel, the joystick, and even the game cartridges.

McLaren MP4/4 & Ayrton Senna

Icons | 10330 | 2024

The MP4/4 was one of the most dominant Formula 1 cars ever built. This is a set that will maintain a place for display on my shelf.

Darth Vadar

Star Wars | 75304 | 2021

The Vader helmet has real presence on a shelf, with its dark finish and iconic silhouette. This was the first LEGO helmet set I purchased, and I keep it on my desk at work.

Disney Castle

Disney | 71040 | 2016

With over 4,000 pieces, this was the set my wife and I built together. This is one that belongs to the whole family, and my kids helped finish it over a long weekend.
